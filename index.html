About The Study

The goal of this project is to identify and analyze fake news surrounding the red-tagging of activist and UPD-DCS alumnus Chad Booc, with the goal of verifying the existence and prevalence of mis/disinformation online.

Motivation

Chad Errol Booc, a Department of Computer Science alumnus and activist, was one of the victims of the ‘New Bataan 5 massacre’, a brutal raid dubbed a "rescue operation". After graduating cum laude, he chose the path less traveled: he volunteered as a mathematics teacher at a school for Lumad children. Accused of being a member of the NPA and recruiting minors for military activity despite his good intentions for the underserved, he has been constantly red-tagged by the military, even after his death.

Deciding on which topic to proceed with was not an easy task. Amongst other instances of online mis/disinformation (e.g., COVID-19 cures, K-12 abolishment), Booc’s case study seemed to be the most sensitive, especially to UPD-DCS students like us, as this involves an individual who must not be dehumanized with numbers. However, given the current political context—where red-tagging has grown more common and deadly as a result of the Anti-Terrorism Act (ATA) of 2020—we have chosen to proceed with this subject with the utmost caution.

Problem

Red-tagging is the act of branding and accusing individuals or groups of being terrorists or communists (Guevarra & Arcilla, 2020). Due to the signing of the Anti-Terrorism Act of 2020, red-tagging has become a more pressing danger. With its vague definition for ‘terrorism’, the ATA allows the government to target human rights defenders and activists, violating the Filipinos’ freedom of expression (Tan, 2022). More crucially, multiple studies and news have shown that there seems to be a correlation between red-tagging and extrajudicial killings in the Philippines (Espiritu, 2022; Gavilan, 2020, Morella et al., 2021).

Solution

With the hopes of increasing awareness about the dangers of red-tagging and gathering knowledge that can aid in the creation of more solutions that can put an end to it, this project aims to verify the existence of red-tagging of Lumad school teacher Chad Booc and quantify its prevalence.

Problem Formulation

Research Question

 What are the multiple reasons Twitter accounts have to red-tag human rights activist Chad Booc and label him as a terrorist or communist?

Hypothesis

 Twitter accounts red-tag Chad Booc because Lumad teachers are accused of terrorist or communist activities.

Null Hypothesis

 There are no apparent reasons for Twitter accounts to red-tag a Lumad teacher.

Action Plan

 Determine and analyze the reasons why Chad Booc is red-tagged by Twitter accounts.
